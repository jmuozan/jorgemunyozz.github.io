In today's quickly evolving world, the preservation of traditional craftsmanship stands at a critical crossroads. This project aims to globalize and modernize the transmission of ancient craftsmanship knowledge through AI. Its primary mission is safeguarding cultural heritage and providing invaluable educational resources for future generations.

NEW MATERIALS AND NEW METHODS

This project investigates the integration of robotic 3D printing with sustainable biomaterials to create innovative architectural structures. It explores the pressing need to reduce carbon emissions in buildings by focusing on both operational and embodied energy. Using a bio-composite cork material derived from recycled cork stoppers, the project explored advanced additive manufacturing methods through robotic fabrication, aiming to improve resource efficiency and minimize waste.

ROBOTIC FABRICATION

The use of robotic arms with multiple axes enhances 3D printing capabilities, allowing the creation of complex geometries and overcoming limitations of traditional layer-based printing, such as support for overhangs and material anisotropy. This setup allows for flexibility in path geometry and custom end-effectors, providing architects, designers and engineers with a powerful tool to expand design possibilities.

GLOBAL KNOWLEDGE

This project in continuously adapting to the changing digital world, trying to revolutionize the way craftsmanship is taught by merging historical methods with modern advancements. The digital platform preserves and teaches ancient skills, celebrating cultural heritage while promoting innovation. The initiative supports "global knowledge, local production," encouraging collaborations among artisans, educators, and learners all over the world. This approach might help local traditions thrive by integrating global insights and technology. The project's goal is to ensure traditional craftsmanship thrives globally, supported by technology and a commitment to preserving culture and providing quality education.

DAFNE +

As part of my role in Horizon funded project Dafne+ Ambassador I've been working in the intersection between the crafts and the use of blockchain technology exposing the project in events and workshops. Currently I'm developing research on how NFTs can be used as certificates in this educational platform that gives value to the different educational crafts courses.

Blockchain will give transparency and inmutability to the certificates. Allowing everyone on the blockchain to validate those. Also will benefit artisans and artists, allowing them to connect with students without intermediaries.
